Data Collection Questions

👤 Best to ask: Marketing Operations or IT Manager

Think about all the places where you run ads - each platform is a separate data source we'll need to connect to.

Examples: Google Ads, Facebook/Meta, LinkedIn, TikTok, Amazon, Twitter, Snapchat

👤 Best to ask: Marketing Operations or Account Manager

Some businesses have multiple accounts on the same platform for different brands, regions, or business units.

Examples: 1 main account, 3 accounts (different regions), 2 accounts (different brands)

👤 Best to ask: Campaign Manager or Media Buyer

Think of all campaigns including seasonal promotions, product launches, etc. but excluding 'Always On' campaigns.

Examples: 50 campaigns/year, 12 monthly campaigns

👤 Best to ask: Creative Director or Media Buyer

Ad Groups/Ad Sets help you organize ads by theme, audience, or targeting criteria within each campaign.

Note: This number may vary so please provide the closest you can to an average

👤 Best to ask: Creative Director or Content Manager

This includes different headlines, descriptions, images, videos, or any creative variations you run for your ad.

Note: This number may vary so please provide the closest you can to an average

👤 Best to ask: Campaign Manager or Marketing Manager

Consider your typical campaign duration - some might be short promotions while others run continuously.

Examples: 7 days (weekly promotions), 30 days (monthly campaigns), 90 days (quarterly)

Additional Requirements

👤 Best to ask: Marketing Manager or Analytics Lead

Think about specific themes beyond individual platforms. Each dashboard contains 15-20 visualizations around a specific topic.

Examples: Regional/Market Analysis, Campaign Pacing, Creative Intelligence Deep-dive

👤 Best to ask: CMO, Marketing Director, or VP Marketing

High-level dashboards designed for leadership, focusing on KPIs and strategic insights.

Examples: Executive Marketing Summary, Monthly Performance Overview, Budget vs Performance

👤 Best to ask: Campaign Manager or Marketing Operations

Patterns extract information embedded in structured naming conventions in Campaign Names, Ad Groups, creatives etc.

Example: Product_Audience_Season

Sample: "Shoes_Women_Summer2024"

Extracts: Product Type, Target Audience, Season

Note: One Pattern is counted for each naming convention, regardless of the number of extractions

👤 Best to ask: Data Analyst or Marketing Operations

Data fields where you see variations of the same value that should be standardized.

Example: Region Normalization

Before: "US", "USA", "us", "AMER"

After: All become "United States"

Example: Device Category Grouping

Before: "iPhone 15", "Samsung S24", "Pixel 8"

After: All become "Mobile Device"

Note: One Enrichment is counted for each Dimension it is applied to, regardless of how many classifications are created with that Enrichment
